The Problem

ADS-B was designed as a broadcast bulletin board.
It is now critical infrastructure.

Every commercial aircraft yells its position over 1090 MHz, unencrypted and unsigned. Air traffic control, airline operations, search-and-rescue, and the public map all read from the same shouting room.

A modern airliner transmits its identity, position, altitude, speed, and intent roughly every half-second, in plaintext. The protocol was finalised in 1997 with a security assumption that has not survived the SDR era: that nobody could afford the radio to fake it.

That assumption is now wrong by three orders of magnitude. A working ADS-B transmitter costs $300. A working aggregator-feeder push costs $0 — anyone with a feeder credential can write rows directly into the production database that FlightAware, FR24, ADS-B Exchange and OpenSky read from.

The result: every major aggregator has been spoofed at least once. Sometimes the attack lasts an hour and 58,000 viewers watch a destroyed aircraft fly. Sometimes the attack lasts five minutes, gets noticed by three Reddit users, and is silently scrubbed from history. Sometimes nobody notices at all.

Threat Taxonomy

Three attack shapes. Each costs the attacker different things.

Wrongness has multiple paths to the screen. The detection problem is the same: a track is displayed that does not correspond to a real aircraft moving as displayed.

Class 1 · Ghost Injection

An aircraft that doesn't exist.

The attacker fabricates every field from code. Position, altitude, speed, callsign, hex — all synthesised. Broadcast over RF, or pushed through a feeder.

Example: VANCE Mar-a-Lago, Jan 28-29 2026. 6,239 fabricated positions. Drew the word "VANCE" across Palm Beach in raster lines. Used AF1's hex `adfdf9`. 58k viewers.
Class 2 · Diversion / Modification

A real flight, with the data rewritten in-flight.

A real aircraft is broadcasting normally. The attacker intercepts, modifies, or overrides position, altitude, callsign — leaving everything else real. The displayed flight diverges from physical truth.

Example: THY580 Istanbul→Taif, Jan 26 2026. Two consecutive messages teleport the aircraft 270 km, then back. Implied speed: Mach 17. Altitude, gs, track all preserved.
Class 3 · Replay

An old flight, rebroadcast as new.

Captured frames from a previous flight (yours or someone else's) are emitted again, with or without modifications. The trajectory looks plausible because it's a real one — from yesterday, or last year, or another country.

Example: FCKPUTIN — AN-225 "Mriya" hex `508035` broadcasting 12 days after the airframe's destruction in Ukraine. Confessed by author with full toolchain published.

Stakes

Why this matters now.

The cases that make the news are pranks. The cases that don't aren't.

ATC and emergency response read the same data.

Air traffic control is the formal source of truth, but the public maps that airlines, dispatchers, and search-and-rescue cross-reference under load read ADS-B aggregators. A poisoned aggregator feed during an in-flight emergency is a force-multiplier for confusion.

VIP and military movements are exposed.

The same broadcast layer that the VANCE attacker used to draw graffiti can be used to track real military and head-of-state aircraft. Several state operators now deliberately broadcast misleading hex codes for OPSEC — but defenders cannot distinguish that from a third-party spoof if they cannot read the data layer carefully.

One feeder credential = global narrative.

The cases above repeat one pattern: a single rogue feeder, often with falsified location and disabled MLAT, propagates to every aggregator that doesn't filter at ingest. FR24 froze new-feeder onboarding for three weeks after FCKPUTIN. That is the entire industry's current containment posture.

The detection precedent is the deterrent.

Today there is no operational system the aggregators run to flag injection on the data layer. They mitigate post-hoc, by scrubbing the historical record and hoping few people noticed. Tracer's POC is a system they can deploy directly, in their own pipeline, that names what it sees.

Approach

The world is consistent. The attacker is not.

A real aircraft satisfies a set of physical invariants simultaneously — physics, atmosphere, RF source, history. A synthesised track satisfies only the ones the attacker remembered to forge.

The architecture is not a stack of ad-hoc rules. It is derived from one observation about the asymmetry of the problem: "Each invariant the world enforces is one more thing the attacker has to fake — and each costs them different effort. Detection becomes: test the invariants the attacker didn't."
I₁

Physics

Position is the integral of velocity. Velocity changes are bounded by aerodynamic acceleration. Altitude moves with vertical rate. A 19,000 ft altitude change in 22 seconds is not an airframe.

Attacker cost to satisfy: trivial. Most lazy attackers still fail it.

I₂

Aircraft Envelope

Each airframe has a type, a ceiling, a Vmo, a service speed. A CRJ-200 at FL360 / Mach 0.95 is outside its envelope. A Cessna at FL400 is outside its envelope.

Attacker cost to satisfy: low. Stay within sensible bounds.

I₃

Atmosphere

Every aircraft in the same airspace at the same altitude experiences the same wind, temperature, and pressure. We use peer aircraft as opportunistic sensors of the atmosphere they share.

Attacker cost to satisfy: medium. Requires real-time METAR / winds-aloft to forge. Most don't bother.

I₄

RF Source

Each RF transmission comes from one location. Multiple receivers triangulate the source via MLAT. A claimed position must equal the actual emission point. Aggregator-layer injections fail this.

Attacker cost to satisfy: high. Requires colocation of a real transmitter with the claimed trajectory.

I₅

Universal Visibility

Real RF propagates omnidirectionally. Every aggregator with a receiver in range should see it. An injection that appears only at FR24 and nowhere else is a fingerprint of the channel.

Attacker cost to satisfy: very high. Requires pushing to every aggregator consistently and simultaneously.

I₆

History

Real aircraft have continuous lifecycles. The hex maps to a registry. The registry says whether the airframe is intact or destroyed. The trajectory shape is similar — or not — to past flights by this hex.

Attacker cost to satisfy: medium. Requires synthesising a complete history.

I₇

Data-Layer Completeness

Real transponders broadcast Mode-S Comm-B downlinks that aggregators decode into derived fields: wind, temperature, true airspeed, autopilot state. Injection scripts don't.

Attacker cost to satisfy: high. Discovered in this research at >100× signal separation.

+

Cross-day, cross-region

Identity collisions — the same callsign on the same hex appearing in distant geographies on different days with totally different data-layer profiles — are unforgeable signatures of either default-hex collisions or deliberate identity copying.

Layer above the axes: meta-signal on the case base.

The math, in one box.

Each axis is an independent hypothesis test. It emits a log-likelihood ratio (LLR) for "fake" vs "real," weighted by data confidence on that flight.

log-posterior(fake) = log-prior + Σᵢ confidenceᵢ · LLRᵢ

The prior is heavily negative — base-rate of attacks is ~1 in 10⁵ to 10⁷ flights. To overcome it, the sum of axis evidence has to be large. That forces the attacker into a multi-axis evasion problem. Each invariant they satisfy costs them something; each they don't costs them detection. The system raises the bar measurably and is honest about which axes carried the verdict on each case.

Per-axis distributions are calibrated separately within coverage strata (dense CONUS, dense Europe, sparse oceanic, war-zone) because regional coverage shapes the noise floor of each axis. Confounded pairs — where two axes are correlated by a shared environmental cause — are factored before fusion. Diversion attacks are caught by per-axis online change-point detection, which fires when multiple axes shift coincidently in time within a single flight.

Invariants

Seven invariants. Each one the attacker has to fake.

Physics, aircraft envelope, atmosphere, RF source, universal visibility, history, data-layer completeness. Each is an independent hypothesis test that costs the attacker different effort to satisfy. Drag the modules — they're alive.
